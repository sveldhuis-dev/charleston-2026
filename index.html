Rental Car vs. Rideshare

For 6 people over 4 days, with a mix of walkable dinners and 2–3 day trips that require wheels. Numbers based on current Charleston rates.

Option A

🚗 Rental Car + Downtown Parking

Rental (4 days, mid-size SUV): ~$220–280
Downtown garage (4 days × $24 max): ~$96
Airport dropoff/pickup (already have): $0
Est. Total: ~$320–380
  • Freedom to leave anytime for day trips
  • Cheaper than 6 Uber rides to North Charleston
  • No surge pricing on July 4th weekend
  • Street meters free after 6 PM (dinners cost $0)
  • Sundays & holidays: all street parking free
  • Downtown parking is a daily hassle
  • No "in and out" at garages — leave and pay again
  • Driver can't drink at dinners
  • King and Queen Garage: $24/day max (most expensive)
Option B — Recommended

📱 Rideshare Only (Uber/Lyft)

Airport transfers (2 × ~$35 × 2 vehicles): ~$140
Day trip to Angel Oak + Boone Hall: ~$60 rt
Day trip to Folly Beach (2 cars): ~$80 rt
Jackrabbit Filly Fri (2 cars × $25): ~$100 rt
Misc evening rides (est.): ~$60
Est. Total: ~$440
  • Everyone drinks at every dinner — no DD needed
  • No parking stress in Historic District
  • Drop off door-to-door at every restaurant
  • No rental car hassle at airport on 5:45 AM departure
  • DASH trolley + walking covers most of downtown
  • July 4th surge pricing likely — pre-book return from fireworks
  • 6 people needs 2 cars for every ride
  • Less flexible for spontaneous day trips
💡 Verdict for this trip: Rideshare-only is likely the better call for a group of 6 on a 4-day trip centered on walkable downtown Charleston. Three of your four dinners are a short walk from Mills House. The only real driving days are Thursday (optional Boone Hall/Angel Oak) and Friday (Jackrabbit Filly in North Charleston). The ability for everyone to drink freely at all four dinners over a holiday weekend is worth the ~$60 premium over a rental.

However: If you're doing Boone Hall + Angel Oak + Folly Beach all in separate day trips, a rental car for just 2 of the 4 days (~$120–140) might make sense as a hybrid — rent for day trips, Uber for the rest. Check rates at CHS airport closer to departure.

☀️ Sunscreen and hats essential — the grounds are exposed and July in SC is genuinely intense.

Insider Knowledge

Local Tips & Advice

🌡️
Work With the Heat Active sightseeing 8–11 AM, air-conditioned lunch / downtime 1–4 PM, evenings back outside. The Battery and Waterfront Park are genuinely pleasant after 6 PM with the harbor breeze.
🌧️
Daily Afternoon Thunderstorms Almost guaranteed in July — typically 30–45 min and then clear. Don't cancel plans, just duck into a bar or café. Part of the Charleston summer rhythm.
🚌
DASH Trolley — Free Route 211 (Green) covers Meeting & King, Waterfront Park to City Market. Runs 9 AM–8 PM Sat. Bus drivers are notably friendly and helpful with local tips.
📱
July 4th Rideshare Surge Pre-book your return from fireworks before you leave for High Cotton dinner. Surge pricing on a holiday Saturday night near downtown fireworks can be significant. Lyft and Uber both allow scheduled rides.
🧺
Sweetgrass Baskets The Gullah Geechee weavers at City Market practice an art form passed down for generations — genuine heirlooms, not souvenirs. Budget $60–200+ for a quality piece. Watching the weaving process is worth as much as the purchase.
🍽️
Group Dining Tip For 6 people, confirm your party size when you arrive at each restaurant. FIG and Husk in particular move tables for large parties — arriving together and on time helps enormously. Jackrabbit Filly is best ordered family-style — let the server guide you on quantity.
📸
Best Photo Spots Rainbow Row at midday, the Battery at dusk, Angel Oak early morning, Waterfront Park at golden hour, Nathaniel Russell spiral staircase, Boone Hall Avenue of Oaks at any time.
💤
The July 5 Morning Set 3 alarms (4:45, 5:00, 5:15 AM). Two cars for 6 people to CHS — pre-book both rideshares the night before. Bags staged at the door. 5:45 AM departure from hotel to make 6:55 AM boarding. Nonstop CVG — worth every bit of planning.
